This document is to explain each status of Jira tickets

  1. Backlog
    The tickets in this status are ones that newly created. The Requirement might not be confirmed. We might know that there should be the work on this feature, but the detail of the feature is not ready yet.
    Action by: Anyone

  2. Requirement Confirmed
    This status means the requirement is solid and the details are confirmed, ready to get into refinement. Acceptance criteria must be stated.
    Action by: K’Kuk, K’Beam, K’Pink

  3. In Progress
    Once the ticket is on the sprint board and one of the team member start developing on the ticket. The ticket should be moved to “In Progress”.
    Action by: Ones who work on the item

  4. QA
    This status is for development quality assurance. After development is done. Developers must move this ticket to QA to have testers test on the ticket according to acceptance criteria in the ticket.
    Action by: Ones who work on the item

  5. In Review
    This status is for Business and PM review the work before releasing out to UAT by 7-11. When the quality check is done, testers must move the ticket to be reviewed, and change assignee to Yanawee. Yanawee will check the work and assign to K’Beam to review to confirm that it is ready for UAT.
    Action by: QA to have the story reviewed, and Yanawee who is the first review assigning to Sertis team

  6. Done
    The stories are reviewed the reviewer should move the story to “Ready for Staging” for the dev to know that it has passed Review and ready for UAT.
    Action by: Sertis team

After this, the dev will release the release build according to the fixed version of each ticket.

 

Notes:

Assignee

Everyone in the team must change assignee to the next person once your part is done. So that, we know who is handling the work at that time. Also, when changing the Assignee, the person should make a note to the assignee in comment. So that, they know what needs to be done on their part for that ticket assigned.

 

Environment

Dev: to internal development, testing, review.

Staging: for UAT purpose, need to discus to with other 7-11 PMs to sync deployment

Prod: need to discus to with other 7-11 PMs to sync deployment
